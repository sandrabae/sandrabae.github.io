Understanding Fisheries

  • Role

    Developer, Designer

  • Location

    Davis, California

  • Year

    2018

Understanding Fisheries, developed with National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), teaches the importance and complexities of fisheries with respect to its linkages socially, economically, and environmentally. This lead to questions such as: which ecosystems become vulnerable to degradation by people? How can better planning help? How can we express these concepts to the public or government officials, so they can understand its significances?

The topic of fisheries was chosen for three reasons: (1) fisheries are generally depicted very colloquially with a flippant notion of boats, the sea, and fish; (2) with the recent discussion and concern about climate change, it is among everyone’s interest and responsibility to be aware of the environment and the detrimental effects we are causing to the planet; (3) when trying to learn more about the complexities of fisheries there is no single data source that fully expresses the fisheries’ relationships with respect to other linkages.

This becomes a giant challenge when trying to raise awareness when the current standard is expected to find the connections between multiple data sources rather than just one. This can easily become overwhelming to discern the communication among the datasets. So, based on research papers that I’ve read and domain experts I have talked to, I accomplished creating a socio-economic network of fisheries that shows the necessary, detailed relationships with respect to its linkages. Using the network, I developed a public/educational tool to inform the public about the importance of fisheries and NOAA’s work. This project should help reinforce the user’s knowledge about the fishery network.

Because this project was framed as a public/educational, it was important that the everything from the design to the first look and feel made an impression to the public. As a general rule, public exhibitions only have few seconds to make an impression to capture the user’s attention. Approaching with a similar method, I have used my design skills to capture the user’s attention by creating dynamic movements on the webpage, using vibrant colors against a dark background to create a strong contrast, and well-design icons. It was also important to create design supplements that help supports the tone of the project

After showing my contact in NOAA my current implementation, she was very impressed and pleased with the design. So currently, I am seeking to further develop it over the summer in hopes of being an actual public exhibition for NOAA. After discussing possible next steps, I will iterate more on the current version and develop a proper education visualization and thoroughly evaluate its effectiveness.
