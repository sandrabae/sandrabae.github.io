Sit on Data

  • Role

    Developer, Designer

  • Location

    Albuquerque, New Mexico

  • Year

    2019

This is a work in progress. A scale model was presented at Imagining America's 20th National Gathering. The purpose of this exhibition is to bring awareness to the systematic issues of climate change. Intended for non-experts, the experience should foster a curiosity for more learning. To do so, this project aims to create a data-physicalization where people can feel the raw data and have the ability to discuss it with others.

DATA PHYSICALIZATION

Data is powerful as it has the ability to present facts, and—if done well—nothing more. If data is collected and analyzed properly, a visualization can share insights absent of bias or zeal.

For this project, we chose to present data in its physicalized form. Jansen et al. 2015 have listed the benefits of data physicalization, such as engagement, accessibility, and multi-modal perceptual exploration skills.

Using Berkeley Earth's data, our project tells the story of the Earth's average temperature since the 1880's. Average temperature is increasing at an alarming rate. Each "ridge" in the average temperature plot can be represented as a slice of the parametric bench. The shape of the parametric bench can show how the world’s average temperature is changing.

We aim to let the viewer feel the gravity of the situation by letting them sit on different shapes of the bench. We aim to see how their feeling of comfort changes over time.
